# Insight 1

Observation:
 Customers with longer contract durations show substantially lower churn rates.
 Month-to-month customers have the highest churn, while two-year contracts have the lowest.

Interpretation:
 Contract commitment appears strongly associated with retention.
 Customers with short-term agreements have more flexibility to leave.

Business Impact:
 * Encourage migration toward longer contracts through incentives such as:
 * discounted pricing
 * loyalty rewards
 * bundled services

Model Impact:
 Contract is expected to be a high-impact predictive feature

# Insight 2

Observation:
 Customers using fiber optic service exhibit significantly higher churn than DSL users.
 Customers without internet service show the lowest churn.

Interpretation:
 Fiber customers may have higher expectations or experience issues related to pricing or service quality.

Business Impact:
 Investigate fiber customer experience:
  * pricing
  * reliability
  * onboarding
  * support quality

Model Impact:
 InternetService should be retained.

# Insight 3

Observation:
 Customers paying through electronic check have noticeably higher churn.

Interpretation:
 Payment method may reflect customer behavior patterns rather than directly causing churn.

Business Impact:
 Encourage migration to automatic payment methods.

Possible actions:
 * cashback
 * discounts
 * easier setup

Model Impact:
 PaymentMethod may provide moderate predictive value
